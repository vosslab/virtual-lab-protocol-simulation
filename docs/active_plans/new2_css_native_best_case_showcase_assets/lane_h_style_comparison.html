Lane H: Visual style direction comparison

Three visual treatments of the same canonical bench scene. Layout, grid, and footprint sizes are identical across all three; only colors, typography, borders, and decorative surfaces differ.

Recommendation: clean_instructional Adopt clean_instructional for the NEW2 showcase. It surfaces the region structure (rear shelf, work surface, front tools, instrument station) without competing with the colored equipment SVGs. The baseline lab_bench_realistic hides region boundaries inside the wood-grain texture, and high_contrast_diagnostic dominates the eye so strongly that the equipment becomes secondary. The blue-on-white palette also reads as a teaching aid rather than a videogame environment, which matches the protocol-authoring intent.

baselinelab_bench_realistic

Warm wood-grain bench surface with faint hairline dividers. Atmospheric and physical, but region boundaries and labels are nearly invisible at the showcase viewport. Works best as an in-scene immersive treatment, less well as a documentation or instructional asset.

recommendedclean_instructional

White ground with soft blue accents, visible slab-serif region labels, and chip-styled placement labels. Equipment SVGs sit in light cards that read as instructional callouts. Region structure is legible at a glance; nothing competes with the equipment colors.

accessibilityhigh_contrast_diagnostic

Yellow-and-black diagnostic palette with sharp 90-degree edges and mono region label chips. Maximum legibility for region structure and label text. The hazard-tape border reads as a debug or QA overlay; suitable as an accessibility mode or a diagnostic capture style, not as the default instructional surface.
