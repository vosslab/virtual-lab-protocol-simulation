Notes on column semantics:

- Hard fails is the precheck `hard_fail_count` sum across the four
  classes `clipped_artwork`, `off_page`, `svg_svg_overlap`, and
  `region_overflow`. See
  [`DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md`](../../../../experiments/css_native_layout/DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md)
  section "Metric reference" entries 1 through 4.
- No-crop failures (clipped_artwork) is one specific hard-fail class.
  The two advisory no-crop sub-checks
  (`artwork_integrity.clipped_by_parent` and
  `artwork_integrity.aspect_distorted` non-HARD\_FAIL) are NOT folded
  into this column because they are advisory in the canonical baseline;
  see section 7 "Known gaps".
- Placeholder / fallback object count is a textual count of
  `_placeholder.svg` references in the scene HTML, sourced from the
  stress-renderer convention noted in
  [ONBOARDING.md](ONBOARDING.md) section 6.
- Label failures is the sum of `label_label_overlap` plus
  `svg_label_overlap` advisory-WARN entries
  (see [`DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md`](../../../../experiments/css_native_layout/DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md)
  metrics 5 and 6). The committed canonical baseline summarizes these
  per scene as "Notable warn flags" rather than as raw counts; the table
  below preserves that summary plus the boolean trigger (`labels_readable`,
  `supporting_nearby`) reported in
  [`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
  section 6.
- Scorecard result is the NEW1.5 Lane C revised-weights score on a
  0-100 scale per scene. The aggregate 632 / 1000 cited in
  [ONBOARDING.md](ONBOARDING.md) section 5 is the raw sum across 10
  scenes (sum equals 712 with the revised weights; the 632 figure comes
  from the pre-Lane-C run and is preserved in
  [`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
  section 12); see section 7 "Known gaps".

## 7. Known gaps

The following gaps are reported in the committed evidence and must be
preserved in any successor diagnostic baseline.

### 7.1 No-crop advisory versus hard fail

Source:
[`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
section 7 "No-Crop Hard Rule" and
[`DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md`](../../../../experiments/css_native_layout/DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md)
metric 12 ("artwork_integrity").

- `clipped_artwork` is a HARD FAIL class (hard-fail gate forces score
  to 0).
- `clipped_by_parent` is currently ADVISORY (WARN). Sub-check on
  ancestor `overflow` clipping is computed but does not increment
  `hard_fail_count` in the canonical baseline; the proposed Phase 1
  hardFailCount semantic that would fold it into the hard-fail gate is
  PROPOSAL (awaiting user decision per
  [`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
  section 7 "Phase 1 Proposal").
- `aspect_distorted` is ADVISORY WARN by default, but the precheck
  escalates to HARD FAIL for object names matching the glassware,
  pipette, plate, or instrument pattern groups (see
  [`DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md`](../../../../experiments/css_native_layout/DIAGNOSTICS_REFERENCE.md)
  "Hard-fail escalation groups").
- The contract rule in
  [`docs/PRIMARY_DESIGN.md`](../../../PRIMARY_DESIGN.md) "Visual
  integrity: never crop scientific assets" applies regardless of
  `hard_fail_count`. Visible cropping is a hard-rule violation even
  when bbox checks report 0 hard fails.

Implication for the baseline table in section 2: the "no-crop failures
(clipped_artwork count)" column reflects only the hard-fail class, not
the advisory `clipped_by_parent` and non-escalated `aspect_distorted`
sub-checks. The next manager should not interpret 0 in that column as
contract compliance; per the design doc the rule binds independent of
the diagnostic gate.

### 7.2 Uncalibrated 632 / 1000 aggregate score

Source:
[`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
section 10 ("Scorecard Status") and section 12 (timeline) plus
[ONBOARDING.md](ONBOARDING.md) section 5.

- The 632 / 1000 aggregate cited as the NEW2 best-case showcase
  scorecard total is the raw sum across 10 scenes from the
  pre-Lane-C-revision run. With Lane C revised weights the per-scene
  scores in section 2 sum to 712; the 632 figure is preserved for
  trail.
- The 0-100 per-scene metric is not calibrated against any external
  reference. Per
  [`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
  section 10 "Current Gaps", the score is a relative comparison knob,
  not an absolute quality target.
- The hard-fail gate (any hard fail forces score 0) is reliable; the
  score number is not. The next manager should keep the hard-fail gate
  and treat the score as advisory.

### 7.3 Stress versus production divergence

Source:
[`current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md`](../../../archive/plan-reset-2026-05-22/current_css_native_layout_manager_status_report.md)
section 8 ("Stress Corpus") and
[ONBOARDING.md](ONBOARDING.md) section 5.

- Stress corpus renders via static-HTML precheck (Playwright on
  isolated HTML), not the production runtime; no React lifecycle, no
  CSS-native adapter integration with ObjectStateChange.
- 100% FAIL across 100 synthetic scenes is evidence of stress-case
  robustness, not production failure.
- Production templates (10 hand-authored) report 0 FAIL / 4
  PASS_TEMPLATE / 6 WARN at 1200x900; only `well_plate_96_zoom` has a
  committed production-viewport run (1920x1080, hard_fail_count = 2).

Implication: stress numbers must not be cited as production proof. The
production-viewport gap for the other 9 templates is a real open item;
the next manager should treat that as a measurement question, not as a
known failure.
