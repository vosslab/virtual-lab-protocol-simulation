# mini-protocol: sdspage attach lid and leads

## Learning

**Objectives.** Students completing this mini-protocol will have achieved fluency with securing electrophoresis equipment by placing the lid on the tank and connecting the high-voltage leads to establish the electrical circuit.

**Outcomes.** Students completing this mini-protocol will be able to place the electrophoresis tank lid in position and attach the red (positive) and black (negative) leads from the power supply to the electrode assembly, ensuring correct polarity orientation for the subsequent gel run.

**Goals.** Overall, this mini-protocol aims to accomplish the final assembly step before electrophoresis: securing the tank and establishing electrical continuity. Once the lid is in place and both leads are attached, the apparatus is ready for voltage application and sample migration.

## Materials

- Tris/Glycine/SDS 1x running buffer
- Used 1x running buffer

## Procedure

### Step 1. Secure apparatus

Secure the electrophoresis apparatus for running the gel. First, place the lid on top of the electrophoresis tank. Then connect the red lead from the power supply to the positive electrode and the black lead to the negative electrode. Ensure both connections are firmly seated.

- The Electrophoresis tank is now lid present: _True_.
- The Power Supply is now cathode lead attached: _True_.
- The Power Supply is now anode lead attached: _True_.
